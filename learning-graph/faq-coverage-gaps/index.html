    • Centrality: High (commonly used congruence theorem)

    • Suggested Question: "What is SAS congruence and how is it different from SSS?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Definition (two sides and included angle), why included angle matters, examples, common mistakes

    3. ASA Congruence

    • Centrality: High (key theorem for many proofs)
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned but no explanation
    • Suggested Question: "What is ASA congruence?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Definition (two angles and included side), relationship to AAS, when to use

    4. AAS Congruence

    • Centrality: Medium-High (useful alternative to ASA)
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned in list
    • Suggested Question: "What is AAS congruence and how does it differ from ASA?"

    5. HL Congruence

    • Centrality: Medium-High (specific to right triangles)
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned in list
    • Suggested Question: "What is HL congruence for right triangles?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Hypotenuse-Leg theorem, why it only works for right triangles, applications

    6. CPCTC

    • Centrality: High (essential for using congruence in proofs)
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What does CPCTC mean and when do I use it?"

    7. Triangle Sum Theorem

    • Centrality: Very High (fundamental property)
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned but no dedicated question

    8. Exterior Angle Theorem

    • Centrality: High (commonly used in proofs)
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What is the Exterior Angle Theorem?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Exterior angle equals sum of remote interior angles, proof, applications

    9. Triangle Inequality Theorem

    • Centrality: High (determines if triangle can exist)
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned in theorem list
    • Suggested Question: "What is the Triangle Inequality Theorem and why is it important?"

    10. Isosceles Triangle Theorem

    • Centrality: High (fundamental property)
    • Current Coverage: None

    11. Base Angles Theorem

    • Centrality: Medium-High (specific to isosceles triangles)
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned but no details
    • Suggested Question: "What are base angles in an isosceles triangle?"

    12. Equilateral Triangle Properties

    • Centrality: Medium (special case of isosceles)

    34. Central Angle

    • Centrality: High (basic circle concept)
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What is a central angle in a circle?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Vertex at center, sides are radii, measures same as intercepted arc

    35. Inscribed Angle

    • Centrality: High (important theorem)
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What is an inscribed angle and how does it relate to the central angle?"

    36. Chord

    • Centrality: Medium-High
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned in glossary
    • Suggested Question: "What is a chord of a circle?"

    37. Tangent to Circle

    • Centrality: High (key circle concept)
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What is a tangent line to a circle?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Touches circle at exactly one point (point of tangency), perpendicular to radius at that point, applications

    38. Secant

    • Centrality: Medium
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned in glossary

    • Suggested Answer Focus: Line intersecting circle at two points, relationship to chords and tangents

    39. Arc Length

    • Centrality: Medium-High (important measurement)
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned but no formula
    • Suggested Question: "How do I calculate arc length?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Formula (/360)2r or r (radians), relationship to circumference, examples

    40. Sector

    • Centrality: Medium
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned but no details
    • Suggested Question: "What is a sector of a circle and how do I find its area?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Pie slice shape, area formula (/360)r, relationship to circle area

    • Centrality: Low-Medium
    • Current Coverage: Mentioned in glossary

    42. Semicircle

    • Centrality: Medium
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What is a semicircle?"

    Triangle Parts and Relationships (6 concepts)

    43. Median of Triangle

    • Centrality: Medium-High
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What is a median of a triangle?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Segment from vertex to midpoint of opposite side, three medians meet at centroid, divides triangle into equal areas

    44. Altitude of Triangle

    • Centrality: Medium-High
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What is an altitude of a triangle?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Perpendicular segment from vertex to opposite side (or extension), used in area formula, three altitudes meet at orthocenter

    45. Perpendicular Bisector of Triangle

    • Centrality: Medium
    • Current Coverage: General perpendicular bisector mentioned
    • Suggested Question: "What is the perpendicular bisector of a triangle side?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Perpendicular line through midpoint of side, three meet at circumcenter, all points equidistant from endpoints

    46. Angle Bisector of Triangle

    • Centrality: Medium
    • Current Coverage: General angle bisector covered
    • Suggested Question: "What is special about angle bisectors in triangles?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Three angle bisectors meet at incenter, Angle Bisector Theorem (divides opposite side proportionally)

    47. Hinge Theorem

    • Centrality: Low-Medium
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What is the Hinge Theorem?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Two triangles with two congruent sides: larger included angle longer third side, converse also true

    48. Third Angle Theorem

    • Centrality: Medium
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What is the Third Angle Theorem?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: If two angles of one triangle are congruent to two angles of another, the third angles are congruent (follows from Triangle Sum)

    Transformation Details (4 concepts)

    • Centrality: High
    • Current Coverage: Defined but could expand
    • Suggested Question: "How do rotations work in geometry?"

    51. Reflection (detailed)

    • Centrality: High
    • Current Coverage: Defined but could expand
    • Suggested Question: "How do I reflect a figure over a line?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Mirror image, perpendicular distance preserved, common lines (x-axis, y-axis, y=x), formulas

    52. Composition of Transformations

    • Centrality: Medium-High
    • Current Coverage: None
    • Suggested Question: "What is a composition of transformations?"
    • Suggested Answer Focus: Performing multiple transformations in sequence, order matters (not commutative), glide reflection

    • Centrality: Medium
