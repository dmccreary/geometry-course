## Why Geometry Matters Now More Than Ever

Geometry is one of the most widely studied subjects in mathematics education worldwide. Here are some statistics that demonstrate its reach:

**In the United States (2026 projections):**

- Approximately **3.5 million high school students** take geometry each year[^1]
- Geometry is a **required course** in nearly every U.S. state for high school graduation[^2]
- Over **95% of high schools** offer geometry, making it far more accessible than advanced math courses[^1]
- Students who complete geometry are significantly more likely to succeed in higher-level STEM courses[^3]

**Worldwide:**

- An estimated **20 million students** study geometry globally each year
- Geometry serves as a foundation for fields including architecture, engineering, computer graphics, robotics, and data visualization

These numbers represent millions of students working to master the same concepts you'll learn in this course. You're joining a global community of learners building the spatial reasoning skills that are essential for understanding the physical world.

## Learning Through Interactive Visualization

This course takes a fundamentally different approach to teaching geometry. Instead of staring at static diagrams, you will build deep intuition through **interactive MicroSimulations**. These browser-based visualizations let you experiment with angles, transformations, constructions, and proofs in real-time.

Drag vertices to see how triangle properties change dynamically. Watch parallel lines create angle relationships before your eyes. Explore how circles intersect and generate beautiful theorems. Manipulate 3D solids and discover how surface area and volume scale with dimensions. These are not passive animations — they are hands-on laboratories where you control the parameters and discover the concepts yourself.
