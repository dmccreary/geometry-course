## About This Diagram

This reference diagram shows the four-panel construction of an angle bisector. The bisector ray BD divides angle ABC into two congruent angles.

## The Four Panels

| Panel | Step | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| **1** | Given | Angle ∠ABC to bisect (≈70°) |
| **2** | Step 1 | Arc from vertex B, mark P and Q |
| **3** | Steps 2-3 | Arcs from P and Q intersect at D |
| **4** | Result | Ray BD bisects ∠ABC |

## Key Properties

- **∠ABD ≅ ∠DBC**: The angle is divided into two equal parts
- **Equal arcs from P and Q**: Same compass radius ensures equal distances
- **Point D determines direction**: The intersection point defines the bisector

## How to Use

1. **Hover over each panel** to highlight and examine
2. **Follow the arc colors** - each step has a distinct color
3. **Notice the congruence marks** in the final result

## Learning Objectives

- **Construct** an angle bisector using compass and straightedge
- **Understand** how the construction creates two congruent angles
- **Apply** the technique to divide any angle in half

**Applying** and **Understanding** - Construction technique application.
