## About This Diagram

This diagram shows reflections over three common lines: the x-axis, y-axis, and the line y = x. Click each button to see the animated reflection.

## Reflection Rules

| Line of Reflection | Coordinate Rule |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| **x-axis** | (x, y) → (x, -y) |
| **y-axis** | (x, y) → (-x, y) |
| **y = x** | (x, y) → (y, x) |

## Visual Elements

- **Red line**: Line of reflection
- **Blue triangle**: Pre-image
- **Green triangle**: Image (reflected)
- **Purple dashed lines**: Perpendicular segments
- **Small squares**: Mark perpendicular intersections

## Key Concepts

- Each point and its image are equidistant from the line of reflection
- The line of reflection is the perpendicular bisector of segments connecting corresponding points
- Orientation reverses (clockwise ↔ counterclockwise)

## Interaction

Click "x-axis", "y-axis", or "y = x" buttons to animate each reflection.

## Learning Objectives

- **Understand** reflection rules for common lines
- **Apply** coordinate transformations for reflections
