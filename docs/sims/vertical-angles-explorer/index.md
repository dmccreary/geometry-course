## About This MicroSim

This interactive demonstration allows you to rotate one of two intersecting lines and observe that **vertical angles always remain equal**, no matter how you rotate the lines.

## Vertical Angles Theorem

> When two lines intersect, vertical angles are congruent.

$$\angle 1 \cong \angle 3$$
$$\angle 2 \cong \angle 4$$

## How to Use

1. **Drag** either blue endpoint to rotate the line
2. **Watch** all four angle measures update in real-time
3. **Notice** that opposite angles (vertical angles) always match
4. **Observe** that adjacent angles always sum to 180°

## Color Coding

| Color | Angles | Relationship |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| Green | ∠1 and ∠3 | Vertical (equal) |
| Yellow | ∠2 and ∠4 | Vertical (equal) |

## Key Observations

- Vertical angles are **always congruent**
- Adjacent angles are **always supplementary** (sum to 180°)
- This works for **any** rotation of the lines

## Learning Objectives

- **Discover** that vertical angles are always congruent
- **Apply** the vertical angles theorem
- **Analyze** the relationship between all four angles
