## About This Diagram

This reference diagram shows the complete construction of a segment bisector in four panels. The construction creates both a midpoint and a perpendicular bisector.

## The Four Panels

| Panel | Step | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| **1** | Given | Segment AB to bisect |
| **2** | Steps 1-2 | Draw arcs from A and B (radius > ½AB) |
| **3** | Step 3 | Mark intersection points P and Q |
| **4** | Result | Draw line PQ - the perpendicular bisector |

## Key Properties

- **Midpoint M**: The intersection of PQ and AB
- **AM ≅ MB**: The segment is divided equally
- **PQ ⊥ AB**: The bisector is perpendicular (90°)
- **Equidistant Property**: Every point on PQ is equidistant from A and B

## How to Use

1. **Hover over each panel** to highlight and examine
2. **Notice the arcs** must have radius greater than half of AB
3. **The result shows** both the midpoint and perpendicular relationship

## Learning Objectives

- **Construct** a segment bisector using compass and straightedge
- **Understand** why the construction creates a perpendicular bisector
- **Identify** the midpoint and its properties
